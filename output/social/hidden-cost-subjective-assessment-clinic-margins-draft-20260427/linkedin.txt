Subjective assessment is quietly eating your clinic's margins.

When physiotherapists estimate strength as "4/5" or pain as "moderate", they introduce variability that ripples through every treatment decision that follows. Clinicians who can't confidently tell a three-session patient from an eight-session patient default to longer courses: defensive over-treatment that looks clinically sound but isn't.

The maths are stark. A practice seeing 200 MSK patients a month, extending average treatment by just 1.5 sessions per patient through measurement uncertainty, loses £5,400 in margin annually, before accounting for re-assessment overhead and unpredictable scheduling. Imprecise initial assessments also force more frequent re-checks to track change, extending appointment times and adding administrative load across the team.

The inconsistency compounds. Some patients get appropriate care, others get significantly more or less than optimal, making outcome prediction and capacity planning unreliable.

Standardise assessment protocols across your team and treatment duration stops depending on which clinician a patient sees. Practices that make the switch typically report margin improvements of 8-15% within six months.
