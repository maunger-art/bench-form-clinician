Subjective assessment variability is a margin problem disguised as a clinical one: when physiotherapists can't confidently differentiate patients needing three sessions from those needing eight, they default to longer courses, extending treatment, driving unpredictable outcomes, and inflating costs without proportional benefit. A practice seeing 200 MSK patients monthly can lose over £5,400 in margin annually purely from this measurement uncertainty, before accounting for the added re-assessment and administrative overhead that imprecise initial assessment creates.
