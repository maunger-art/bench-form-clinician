Subjective assessment isn't a quality problem. It's a margin problem.

When two physiotherapists rate the same patient's strength differently, treatment plans diverge, episodes extend, and nobody can say why. Research comparing standardised rehabilitation protocols found structured approaches cut treatment duration while maintaining outcomes, often the difference between 8 sessions and 12 for the same presentation.

The costs compound. Senior staff spend extra hours reviewing cases and reconciling handovers between practitioners who read the same subjective findings differently. Administrators can't forecast scheduling or discharge dates when timelines vary unpredictably between similar cases.

Patients notice too. Conflicting assessments from different practitioners erode confidence, drive early dropout, and damage referral relationships before a clinic ever sees the numbers.

None of this requires new equipment to fix. Standardising assessment protocols, even with basic tools, closes most of the gap.
