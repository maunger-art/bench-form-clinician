Over 90% of physiotherapists still rely on manual muscle testing, despite its well-documented limitations.

That gap between subjective grading and objective data affects treatment progression, patient outcomes, and how defensible your clinical decisions are. Closing it doesn't require a £3,000 dynamometer.

Structured protocols using single-leg calf raises, plank endurance tests, and band-resisted hip abduction can produce reproducible, clinically useful data, provided positioning, verbal cues, and termination criteria are standardised across every clinician.

Start with 3-4 tests that cover your most common conditions, document the exact protocol, and train staff to run each one identically.
