Objective strength testing doesn't require expensive dynamometers. Structured, standardised protocols using bodyweight tests, resistance bands, and basic equipment can deliver reproducible data that correlates well with gold-standard measures for most clinical purposes. The single-leg calf raise, plank battery, and band-resisted hip abduction tests each provide quantitative and qualitative data supporting better progression decisions and more defensible documentation, provided positioning, cues, and termination criteria stay consistent across every clinician on staff.
