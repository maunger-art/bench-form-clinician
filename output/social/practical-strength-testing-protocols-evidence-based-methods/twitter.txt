[1/5] Over 90% of physios rely on manual muscle testing, despite its documented limitations. You don't need a £3,000 dynamometer to fix that. A short thread on objective strength testing with basic equipment.

---

[2/5] The single-leg calf raise test: reps to fatigue or form breakdown, capped around 25-30. Correlates strongly with plantarflexor strength and predicts functional capacity in athletic and clinical populations.

---

[3/5] For trunk strength, run a battery: prone plank, side plank, and the Sorensen test. Each measures time to fatigue against defined positioning criteria, giving you normative data to guide progression.

---

[4/5] Resistance bands standardise variable resistance for tests like hip abduction. Fix the band at a set distance from the ankle, then measure reps to fatigue or MVC with a handheld dynamometer.

---
