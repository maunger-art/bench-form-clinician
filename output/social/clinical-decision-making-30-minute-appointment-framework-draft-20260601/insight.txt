Objective performance markers predict clinical outcomes more reliably than pain scores or subjective reports. A three-phase structure, baseline testing, targeted intervention, and defined progression criteria, turns a 30-minute appointment into a focused diagnostic window rather than a time constraint. Research on ACL rehabilitation confirms that strength and functional benchmarks predict return to activity better than symptom reports, a principle that extends to routine musculoskeletal caseloads where measurable targets, not impressions, should drive every progression decision.
