Thirty minutes isn't a constraint on good clinical decision-making. It's what forces it.

Structured reasoning beats comprehensive assessment when time is scarce. A three-phase structure works: 5-7 minutes establishing baseline function using 2-3 reproducible tests, roughly 15 minutes of intervention targeted at what those tests reveal, and clear progression criteria defined before the patient leaves the room.

Research on ACL rehabilitation shows objective strength and functional benchmarks predict successful return to activity better than subjective symptom reports (Kotsifaki et al., 2023). The same principle applies to routine MSK caseloads. A baseline of 12 single-leg calf raises progressing to 20 is a 67% improvement that both clinician and patient can track, regardless of what the pain score says that day.

Set the target before the patient leaves, not after you've reviewed your notes.
