Seniority in physiotherapy often functions as a proxy for trust rather than a marker of what a case actually requires. Without standardised baseline and follow-up testing, clinics have no reliable way to verify that a junior or associate clinician is applying a protocol consistently and progressing a patient appropriately, so caseload defaults to the most experienced (and most expensive) staff. Objective, protocol-driven testing narrows that gap, letting routine caseload move to junior clinicians while senior judgement is reserved for atypical presentations.
