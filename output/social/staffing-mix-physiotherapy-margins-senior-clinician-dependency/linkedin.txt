Senior physios shouldn't be carrying caseload a trained associate could manage.

Most UK clinics route anything moderately complex to their most senior (and most expensive) clinician, not because juniors lack technical skill, but because there's no reliable way to verify a protocol is being applied consistently and progressed safely. Seniority becomes a proxy for trust.

That proxy caps how far a clinic can scale without scaling its costliest staff in direct proportion. Evidence from stroke rehabilitation reviews shows structured, protocolised interventions can be delivered effectively below specialist level, provided training, supervision and monitoring are in place. The determining factor isn't seniority, it's whether the delivery model gives a less experienced clinician a reliable structure to follow.

Standardise baseline and follow-up testing, set clear numeric progression thresholds, and any clinician can see where a patient sits against expected benchmarks.
