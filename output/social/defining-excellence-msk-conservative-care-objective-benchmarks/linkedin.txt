Most physiotherapy practices have no idea what 'good' looks like.

Clinicians know they want patients to improve, but improvement stays subjective without objective targets. That makes excellent care impossible to identify, let alone replicate across clinicians and caseloads.

The evidence sets clear benchmarks. Early physiotherapy after total knee arthroplasty gets patients to 90-95% of pre-injury range of motion by 12 weeks, versus 75-80% with delayed rehab. Supervised distal radius fracture programmes deliver 85-90% grip strength recovery at two years, against 70-75% for home exercise alone.

Protocol adherence, not technique choice, separates the two outcomes.

Audit your current outcome data against these published benchmarks before setting practice-wide targets.
