Excellent conservative MSK care is defined by measurement, not technique. Published data sets clear targets: 90-95% pre-injury range of motion by 12 weeks after early TKA rehabilitation, versus 75-80% with delayed intervention, and 85-90% grip strength recovery at two years for supervised distal radius fracture care, versus 70-75% for home programmes. Practices that track outcomes against these benchmarks can identify underperformance early and replicate what works, rather than relying on subjective impressions of patient progress.
