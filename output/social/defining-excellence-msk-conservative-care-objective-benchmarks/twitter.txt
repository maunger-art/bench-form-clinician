[1/5] Most physio practices can't say what 'excellent' outcomes actually look like. The published benchmarks already exist. Here's what they show.

---

[2/5] Total knee arthroplasty: early physiotherapy gets patients to 90-95% of pre-injury range of motion by 12 weeks. Delayed or inconsistent rehab plateaus at 75-80%. (Aftab et al., 2025)

---

[3/5] Distal radius fractures: supervised physio produces 85-90% grip strength recovery vs the unaffected side at two years. Home exercise programmes typically plateau at 70-75%. (Gutiérrez-Espinoza et al., 2024)

---

[4/5] ACL reconstruction: adding open kinetic chain exercises at weeks 2-6 improves strength and function, but only when progression follows objective criteria, not fixed timelines. (Fontanier et al., 2025)
