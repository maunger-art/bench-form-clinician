43% of MSK physiotherapy care doesn't follow current evidence-based guidelines.

This isn't a competence problem. It's a measurement problem: the gaps cluster wherever treatment decisions rely on subjective judgement rather than objective data, in post-surgical rehab, chronic pain management and return-to-sport protocols.

Take ACL reconstruction. Research shows early open kinetic chain exercises can safely accelerate recovery when introduced with the right progression criteria. Many clinicians still avoid them, defaulting to overly conservative protocols. Blood flow restriction training shows the same pattern: strong evidence, inconsistent adoption, often down to uncertainty over pressure and progression parameters.

For clinic owners, this variability is an operational problem as much as a clinical one. Inconsistent care makes staffing harder, pricing less defensible and outcomes harder to justify to commissioners.

The fix doesn't require a separate testing appointment. Build objective assessment into the sessions you already run, starting with the conditions where the evidence-practice gap is clearest.
